  • INSECURITY

    We are a generation that compares, evaluates and judges ourselves with great scrutiny. We are afraid of people’s thoughts about us, we are driven to put ourselves down and this viewpoint affects us. 

    To confront the insecure self, I cast an pendant that shows a child hiding himself under his arm, self assuring himself with his eyes closed, and is surrounded by a rope. The reason why I posture the child with his eyes closed is because children must feel and seen for who they are in order to feel secure.

    And the rope represents the fear itself, it’s not tied on the child, infect it’s just loosely twined around the child’s body. The sculpture shows that the only way for the child to overcome the insecurity is to face his own fear, find it’s cause/origin (the end of the rope), and get rid of it.
